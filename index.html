Strategy Guide: Managing Your Bankroll

📈 When to Move Up

Only move to the next level when your current bankroll exceeds the "Required Bankroll" for that level. This threshold is designed to protect you from a "bad run" of games.

📉 What if I Lose?

If you lose, update your Current Bankroll and add a 4th-6th Place to your history. If your bankroll falls below the requirement for your current level, move back down to the previous level immediately. This discipline is what prevents "ruin."

🔄 Number of Attempts

The "Required Bankroll" essentially tells you how many "bullets" you have. For example, if a level costs $100 and requires a $2,000 bankroll, you are prepared to handle a variance-driven drawdown. Don't think of it as "how many attempts," but rather "am I still above the safety line?"

Comprehensive User Guide & Mathematical Strategy

Overview

This calculator is a professional-grade bankroll management tool designed for Wager-Match Games. It uses real-time statistical analysis to tell you exactly which levels you can play based on your historical performance and risk appetite. The goal is simple: Eliminate your chance of going broke (Ruin) while maximizing your long-term profit.

1. Historical Data & Accuracy

The system calculates your skill level by analyzing your Recent Game History. It converts your 1st, 2nd, and 3rd place counts into a probability distribution.

⚠️ The Sample Size Warning: If you have only played 5-10 games, your stats might be artificially high or low due to luck. To solve this, we recommend a Manual Buffer: Enter your actual stats, but we suggest adding +2 to every category (1st, 2nd, 3rd) and +6 to Losses as a starting base. This gives you a more stable "Safety Net" of 12 games before you trust your own luck too much.

P(pos) = Count(pos) / Total Games

2. Expected Value (EV): Your "Edge"

EV is the most important number in gambling. It identifies if a game is built for you to win or lose in the long run.

  • Positive EV (+EV): You have a skill advantage. Over 1,000 games, you WILL make money.
  • Negative EV (-EV): The house fees and the payouts are working against you. Over time, you WILL lose money.

EV = Σ (Position Probability × Net Profit) - Entry Fee

3. Risk of Ruin (The "Safety Percentage": 5%)

Even a professional player with a huge edge can lose 10 games in a row. This is Variance. The "Risk of Ruin" formula calculates how much cushion you need to survive those losing streaks.

If you set your Risk Tolerance to 5%, it means there is only a 1-in-20 chance that your bankroll will hit $0 before your mathematical edge takes over. Lowering this percentage makes the calculator "stricter," requiring a much larger bankroll before suggesting you move up.

Req. Bankroll = (-ln(Risk) × Variance) / (2 × EV)

4. How to Use the Dashboard

  • Monitor the Recommendation: The dashboard automatically highlights the "Best" level. This is the highest level where you are both profitable (+EV) and safe from ruin.
  • Watch the "Entries" Column: This shows how many "bullets" you have left. If this number turns Orange or Red, you are in the danger zone—move down immediately!
  • The Edit Mode: Use the ⚙️ icon to manually fix your bankroll if you make an error or to adjust the prizes for each level as the game economy changes.
